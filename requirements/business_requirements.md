# Business Requirements - Australian Auto Parts Sales Automation Platform

## 1. Market Analysis & Opportunity

### 1.1 Market Size & Characteristics

**Australian Second-Hand Auto Parts Market**
- **Total Market Size**: $1.5-2 billion annually
- **Number of Businesses**: ~200-250 active wreckers and parts suppliers
- **Geographic Distribution**: 80% concentrated in NSW, VIC, QLD
- **Business Types**:
  - Independent wreckers (60-70%): 3-15 employees, 50-500 vehicles/year
  - Large wrecker chains (15-20%): 50+ employees, 2000+ vehicles/year
  - Specialized dismantlers (10-15%): Focused on specific vehicle types

**Industry Characteristics**
- Highly fragmented market with low technology adoption
- Traditional, manual-intensive operations
- Limited online presence (40% with no web presence)
- Price discovery challenges (no standardized pricing)
- Supply chain inefficiencies

### 1.2 Customer Pain Points

**Operational Inefficiencies**
1. **Manual Inventory Management** (70% of businesses)
   - Spreadsheets or paper-based systems
   - Time-consuming data entry
   - High error rates
   - Difficulty tracking parts across multiple locations

2. **Customer Inquiries & Communication**
   - 50-200 phone calls daily just for parts availability
   - 30-40% of staff time spent physically searching yards
   - Manual follow-ups via phone/SMS
   - No standardized response times

3. **Pricing & Revenue Management**
   - Inconsistent pricing across customer types
   - Manual price updates
   - No competitive price monitoring
   - Revenue leakage from outdated inventory

4. **Customer Experience**
   - No self-service options
   - Long wait times for quotes (1-2 days typical)
   - Limited transparency into order status
   - No online payment options

5. **Compliance & Legal**
   - Manual warranty tracking
   - Difficult returns/refund processing
   - Privacy regulation compliance burden
   - GST/BAS reporting manual process

### 1.3 Competitive Landscape

**Existing Solutions**

| Solution | Type | Strengths | Weaknesses | Price |
|----------|------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| **Car-Part.com.au** | Marketplace | Large network, brand recognition | Commission-based, no inventory mgmt | 10-15% commission |
| **RevolutionParts** | SaaS | Comprehensive, US proven | Expensive, US-focused, poor AU support | $500-800/mo |
| **Excel/Paper** | Manual | Free, flexible | Time-consuming, error-prone, no automation | Free |
| **Generic POS** | Software | Simple, low cost | Not specialized for auto parts | $50-200/mo |
| **Our Platform** | SaaS | AU-first, affordable, comprehensive | New entrant | $99-799/mo |

**Competitive Advantages**
1. **Australian-First Design**: Built specifically for AU regulations, payment systems, shipping
2. **Affordable Tiered Pricing**: $99/month entry point vs. $500+ competitors
3. **Comprehensive Solution**: Inventory + CRM + Orders + Payments in one platform
4. **Mobile-First**: Native apps for iOS & Android (not web-only)
5. **Local Support**: Australian-based customer support team
6. **Compliance Built-In**: Privacy Act, ACL, GST, ABN validation integrated

## 4. Revenue Projections

### 4.1 Customer Acquisition Plan

**Year 1: Launch & Establish (Months 1-12)**
- **Pilot Phase** (Months 4-6): 5-10 beta customers (free/heavily discounted)
- **Soft Launch** (Months 10-11): Gradual onboarding, 5 new customers/week
- **Public Launch** (Month 12): Open registration
- **End of Year 1**: 20-25 paying customers

**Year 2: Growth (Months 13-24)**
- **Growth Targets**: Add 35-40 new customers
- **Churn Assumption**: 5% monthly (85% retention)
- **End of Year 2**: 60 customers

**Year 3: Scale (Months 25-36)**
- **Growth Targets**: Add 90 new customers
- **Churn Assumption**: 5% monthly (maintaining)
- **End of Year 3**: 150 customers

### 4.2 Revenue Model

**Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)**

*Year 1 Progression*:
- Month 1-3: $0 (development)
- Month 4-6: $1,500 (5 beta customers)
- Month 7-9: $3,000 (10 soft launch customers)
- Month 10-12: $6,000 (ramp to 20 customers)
- **Year 1 Total**: $72,000 (ramping)

*Year 2*:
- Base: 20 customers × $299 = $5,980/month
- Growth: +35 new customers by month 24
- Churn: -5 customers (5% monthly)
- End of Year 2: 50 customers = $14,950/month
- **Year 2 Total**: $215,000

*Year 3*:
- Base: 50 customers × $299 = $14,950/month
- Growth: +90 new customers
- Churn: -10 customers (5% monthly)
- End of Year 3: 130 customers = $38,870/month
- **Year 3 Total**: $538,000+

**Add-On Revenue**: ~$10K-15K annually (10% of subscription revenue)

**Blended Average Revenue Per User (ARPU)**
- Year 1: $299 (assuming Pro tier average)
- Year 2: $315 (mix of tiers + add-ons)
- Year 3: $340 (established customer upsells)

### 4.3 Unit Economics

**Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**
- **Pilot Program**: $0 CAC (free access)
- **Referral Program**: $50 credit per referral (estimated $100 cost)
- **Content Marketing**: Assume 10% conversion from blog/YouTube
- **Average CAC**: $1,000-1,500 per customer

**Lifetime Value (LTV)**
- **Average Subscription Duration**: 36 months (3 years)
- **Average Monthly Price**: $320
- **Gross Margin**: 70% (platform margin before support)
- **LTV = $320 × 36 × 0.7 = $8,064**

**LTV:CAC Ratio**: 5-8:1 (healthy economics)

## 8. Risk Analysis & Mitigation

### 8.1 Market Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| **Low customer adoption** | Medium | Critical | Pilot program, iterate on feedback, free trial |
| **Competitor enters market** | Medium | High | Build moat through service quality, fair pricing |
| **Economic downturn** | Low | High | Emphasize ROI, cost-saving focus, flexible pricing |
| **Industry consolidation** | Low | Medium | Target large chains as enterprise customers |

### 8.2 Operational Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| **Development delays** | Medium | High | Phased MVP, buffer time in timeline, outsourcing |
| **Key person dependency** | Medium | High | Cross-training, documentation, team diversity |
| **Scope creep** | High | High | Strict feature prioritization, phased approach |
| **Budget overrun** | Medium | Medium | Fixed-price contracts, milestone-based payments |

### 8.3 Technical Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| **VIN API unavailable** | Medium | High | Multiple fallback decoders, local caching |
| **Payment gateway outage** | Low | High | Support multiple gateways, offline recording |
| **Data security breach** | Low | Critical | Security audits, encryption, insurance |
| **Scalability issues** | Medium | High | Load testing, auto-scaling architecture |
